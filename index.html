CRAP: Conservation-genetic Research on Animals and their Parasites

Integrating laboratory experiments, field studies, and population genomics to conserve wildlife and uncover complex patterns of parasite transmission.

ABOUT US

Welcome to the CRAP Lab at Arkansas State University. My lab focuses on the environmental and ecological factors that affect the genetics and genomics of host and parasite populations. My research has a strong focus on how historic factors have led to the current genetic composition of organisms and how contemporary factors are affecting the evolutionary potential of populations. Many population-level processes can not be readily observed through field observations. Thus, we use genetic and genomic tools to disentangle complex patterns of population structure, gene flow, and parasite transmission. We commonly use nematodes, trematodes, snails, amphibians, and mammals in our research, but our techniques can be easily expanded to other organisms. We integrate field surveys, laboratory experiments, genetics, toxicology, morphometrics, and ecological models to address fundamental and complex evolutionary–ecology questions.
