Phenotypic Plasticity


Snails in the genus Physa inhabit ponds, lakes, and streams, including eutrophic and oligotrophic environments. Additionally, snails from these different environments have different shell morphologies which complicates species identifications. This could be caused by differences in nutrients, water flow, predators, parasites, and others. I’m use genetics, common garden laboratory experiments, and reciprocal transplant experiments between ponds and streams a to determine the influence of the environment on snail morphology.
